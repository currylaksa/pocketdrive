The story starts with a question every driver is about to face →
01 — The Problem

Fuel just stopped being one national price.

Malaysia is mid-transition from blanket subsidies to a tiered system (BUDI95). Diesel has floated with global markets since 2024. RON95 stays subsidized for most — but the top 15% and foreign nationals now pay market rate, with monthly quotas tightening.

For the first time, what fuel costs depends on who you are and how you drive — not a flat national number.

If cost now depends on behaviour, behaviour is where the savings hide →
01

02 — The Insight

Same car. Same road. A 15–20% gap.

Two drivers on the identical commute can see a 15–20% fuel-cost difference — purely from how they drive and when they refuel. That's a lever every driver can pull today, with zero new hardware.

The sensor already rides in their pocket: GPS, accelerometer, a camera for receipts.

So we turned the phone into a co-pilot →
